Watches we evaluate

We evaluate modern and vintage watches, luxury timepieces, precious-metal watches, and watches from estate collections. A watch’s brand, reference, movement, materials, originality, age, condition, and current demand can all affect its value.

Luxury Watches

Fine Swiss and designer watches are considered based on reference, condition, and market demand.

Vintage Watches

Older mechanical and estate watches may have collector value even when they are not currently running.

Gold Watches

Gold cases, bracelets, gemstones, and the complete timepiece are evaluated together.

What to bring with a watch

Keep the watch with its box, warranty card, papers, receipts, extra bracelet links, service records, and replaced original parts when available. Do not polish or repair a vintage watch solely for an evaluation. If sending photos, include the dial, case, clasp, caseback, reference markings, and accessories.
